# Festival Post Playbook

Use this playbook for `festive-greeting`.

## Core job
Deliver a respectful premium greeting poster.

## Asset rules
- Default to project-free festive composition unless project linkage is explicitly requested.
- If a logo is included, keep it small and secondary.

## Core behavior
- Festival-first, not project-first.
- Poster-first, not plain-image-first.
- Premium restraint over loud celebration.
- Controlled multi-color is welcome. Muted does not mean beige-only.
- Do not let every route collapse into the same centered premium card.

## Composition families
- Symbol-led premium greeting
- Invitation-card festive poster
- Minimal festive poster with calm negative space
- Ribbon / band-sweep poster
- Emblem and field poster
- Folk / cultural illustration poster

## Festival-specific visual vocabulary
- Diwali: diya, rangoli, mandala, ceremonial symmetry, ember warmth
- Eid: arches, crescent, sparse geometry, calm sacred framing
- Navratri / Durga Puja: Durga eyes, dandiya rhythm, devotional symmetry
- Holi: controlled color placement, playful but curated movement
- Ganesh Chaturthi: symbolic Ganesha form, sacred minimal framing
- Republic Day / Independence Day: Ashoka Chakra, softened tricolor bands, civic geometry, paper-ribbon movement, restrained patriotic symbolism

## Palette behavior
- Use 3 to 5 coordinated colors when helpful.
- Multiple colors are allowed when they are softened and curated.
- Do not default to dual-tone only unless the brief explicitly asks for a two-tone route.
- Avoid neon, hyper-saturation, glow-heavy patriotic colors, and “all colors at once.”

## Required prompt behavior
- Write it as a finished premium greeting poster with a clear blessing-safe zone and generous negative space.
- Keep the composition project-free by default unless the brief explicitly requests project linkage.
- Use one calm symbolic focal arrangement or one restrained ceremonial motif, not a cluttered festive montage.
- Vary poster logic across routes: do not make every prompt a centered emblem on a dark solid background.
- For national or civic festivals, prefer refined patriotic poster logic over luxury architectural brand-card logic.
- If a logo is included, keep it tiny and secondary in a footer/signature zone.
- Avoid sales overlays, property facts, floor plans, and brochure clutter.

## Must not leak
- Project facts by default
- Property-ad clutter
- Loud kitsch or festival overload
- Same centered premium card for every festival
- Dark background plus one metallic accent as the default festival formula
