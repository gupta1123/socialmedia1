# SKILL.md — Premium Testimonial Prompting

## Purpose

Use this skill to generate prompts for **premium real-estate testimonial social posts**.

The goal is to create prompts that produce images which feel:
- premium
- trusted
- authentic
- social-media-ready
- designed like a polished editorial post, not a generic quote widget

This skill is for:
- testimonial posts
- customer quote posts
- review highlight posts
- trust-building social creatives

---

## Core objective

A good testimonial prompt should create:

**believable quote + readable hierarchy + premium poster treatment**

The image should:
- make the quote the clear hero
- keep attribution credible and secondary
- feel like a designed social post
- remain warm, authentic, and trustworthy

---

## Non-negotiable rules

1. **Prioritize quote readability**
2. **Keep attribution clean and secondary**
3. **Design it as a social poster, not a quote widget**
4. **Make the quote dominant at thumbnail size**
5. **Use clear quote zone and attribution safe area**
6. **Do NOT invent review badges, star ratings, or platform UI**
7. **Do NOT overstuff with sales claims**
8. **Keep background imagery quiet and secondary**

---

## What good outputs should feel like

- premium testimonial poster
- polished Instagram / social media creative
- authentic, trusted, and believable
- editorial and warm
- modern and restrained

They should **not** feel like:
- generic quote card widget
- sales brochure
- fake review badge
- star rating system
- cluttered compliance ad
- stock testimonial photo

---

## Asset rules

- Prioritize quote readability over decorative imagery.
- Keep attribution hierarchy clean and secondary to the message.
- If a portrait or interior support image is used, keep it secondary and quiet.
- Do not invent review badges, star ratings, or customer-platform UI.
- Do not overstuff with sales claims or legal compliance.
- Use safe, believable attribution: customer name, role/relationship, project context if available.

---

## Prompting workflow

### 1. Define quote composition
Pick one approach:
- quote as hero (large quote text dominates)
- quote with quiet background (subtle project/interior)
- quote with subtle portrait (human authenticity)
- editorial quote card (clean minimal design)

### 2. Define framing
Pick one:
- clean typographic focus
- quote + quiet architectural backdrop
- quote + subtle human presence
- premium quote card design

### 3. Define design treatment
The image should read as:
- premium testimonial poster
- designed social-media post
- polished quote highlight
- editorial review feature

Use:
- clear quote zone
- clean attribution area
- premium containment
- one design gesture only

### 4. Define mood
Prefer:
- warm authentic tone
- clean premium light
- quiet professional
- trusted and believable

Avoid:
- overly salesy language
- fake endorsement graphics
- loud star badges
- cluttered compliance

## Brand Colors & Typography (MANDATORY)

- Apply the brand palette (primary, secondary, accent, neutrals) to typography, quote elements, attribution text, and graphic accents.
- Use the brand's exact headline font family for the quote and attribution text.
- Typography must feel premium and readable - the quote should be the hero, with text integrated into the design, not pasted on top.
- Brand colors should unify the testimonial scene with the text.
- When describing text in the scene, use the brand's exact font style (e.g., "bold Gotham sans-serif quote text" or "refined serif attribution").

---

## Text / Messaging Rules (MANDATORY)

Testimonial posts use the provided quote and attribution.

Use:
- exact quote from the brief if provided
- safe generic testimonial language when no exact copy
- clean attribution: name, role, relationship

Typical text layers may include:
- quote text
- customer name
- role or relationship to project
- project name if relevant

Do not invent:
- fake star ratings
- invented review badges
- platform logos not in brief
- offers or pricing
- legal claims
- customer details not provided
- unit types or project details

If no exact quote is given, keep messaging generic and believable:
- "We've found our perfect home."
- "The best decision we ever made."
- "Exactly what we were looking for."
- "A seamless experience from start to finish."
- "Home truly feels like home."

---

## Logo Integration (when enabled)

- Logo must integrate seamlessly with the testimonial design - NOT look like a sticker on top.
- Never place logo on a white/solid background - use transparent logo that blends naturally with the scene.
- Position subtly in a footer corner or signature zone where it contrasts naturally with the image.
- The logo should enhance brand recognition without distracting from the testimonial quote.

---

## Default prompt template

```text
Create a premium real-estate testimonial social poster with the quote as the dominant visual element. Prioritize quote readability above all else - the quote text should be large, clear, and easy to read at thumbnail size.

The result should read as a designed testimonial poster rather than a generic quote widget. Add a refined graphic design treatment with clear hierarchy, a clean attribution zone, and a polished premium finish. Keep the background quiet and secondary - whether it's a subtle architectural backdrop, subtle human presence, or clean typographic focus.

Use warm, authentic tone with trust-building mood. Use clean daylight or soft premium light. Avoid fake review badges, invented star ratings, sales brochure clutter, or platform UI. The attribution should feel believable: customer name, role, and relationship to the project.

Avoid over-decorated backgrounds, cluttered sales claims, fake endorsement graphics, or any distortion of the testimonial's authentic voice.
```

## Variation levers

To avoid repetitive testimonial posts, vary these:

**Composition:**
- quote as hero (text dominant)
- quote + quiet architectural backdrop
- quote + subtle human presence
- editorial quote card (clean minimal)

**Design intensity:**
- ultra-minimal typography focus
- stronger quote panel
- quiet graphic accent
- premium template feel

**Mood:**
- warm authentic
- professional trust
- quiet premium
- editorial elegance

---

## Quick repair rules

**If the quote isn't readable:**
- Reinforce: quote as priority, large clear text, readability first

**If the output feels too salesy:**
- Add: authentic voice, believable tone, avoid sales claims

**If the background is too busy:**
- Add: quiet background, restraint, premium whitespace

**If it feels too generic:**
- Change: composition, design intensity, mood

---

- Fake review badges or invented star systems
- Customer-platform UI or logos

- Cluttered sales claims
