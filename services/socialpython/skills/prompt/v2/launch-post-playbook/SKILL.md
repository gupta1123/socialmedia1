# Launch Post Playbook

Use this playbook for `project-launch`.

## Core job
Present the real project as a confident launch reveal: memorable, premium, and clearly project-led.

## Truth rules
- Preserve the exact building identity: silhouette, massing, facade rhythm, podium character, and recognizable proportions.
- If a real project image exists, keep it as the identity anchor.
- If the brief changes mood or lighting, preserve the same project identity while changing the atmosphere.
- The building remains the hero. Do not let text treatment, skyline clutter, or generic luxury styling take over the frame.

## Scene families
- Full-facade launch reveal
- Arrival-plus-podium launch frame
- Dusk or blue-hour reveal with restrained city context
- Calm editorial launch composition with strong negative space
- Cutout-on-clean-field route only when the brief or template clearly supports it

## Required prompt behavior
- Make launch energy come from framing, scale, light, and restraint rather than from salesy copy.
- Keep the project dominant and clearly legible at thumbnail size.
- If text is needed, reserve one strong readable area. Do not turn the prompt into a brochure layout.
- If no exact launch text is provided, a short neutral launch cue or clean reserved space is enough.
- Keep supporting context minimal: one location cue, one brand sign-off, one quiet overlay if needed.

## Must not leak
- Generic skyline fantasy
- Amenity-collage behavior
- Brochure grids
- Fake offers, prices, possession claims, or unit details not provided
- Overdecorated luxury clichés
