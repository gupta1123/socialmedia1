# Brand Compliance Interpreter

Use this skill after reading brand truth.

## Required outputs
- Hard rules
- Soft preferences
- Banned claims and patterns
- Logo / QR usage rules

## Rules
- Convert brand guidance into image behavior, not prose repetition.
- Resolve conflicts in this order: exact asset contract, exact required text, compliance and factual bans, selected playbook, project or festival truth, brand hard rules, brand soft preferences, variation styling.
- Use the saved brand palette as the default graphic system. Primary color controls typography or dark overlays, secondary color controls neutral backgrounds or light panels, and accent color controls thin dividers, small highlights, or restrained emphasis.
- Do not default to generic orange/golden sunset styling unless the saved palette or brief clearly supports it.
- For `festival-post-playbook`, treat brand guidance as finish control, not subject control. Palette, typography mood, and imageTreatment may refine the poster, but they must not force a luxury interior, architecture-led, or photoreal still-life route unless the brief explicitly asks for one.
- For `festival-post-playbook`, if the saved imageTreatment suggests realism, translate that into finish quality and material restraint, not camera-shot language or lifestyle staging.
- For `festival-post-playbook`, the cultural or national festival palette may extend beyond the strict saved brand palette. The brand palette should act as compatibility guidance, typography control, or subtle accent logic, not as a hard limiter that suppresses culturally appropriate 4-5 color festive palettes.
- Keep banned claims and banned patterns explicit.
- Treat logo and QR as exact assets, never model-generated substitutes.
- Keep logo treatment secondary, integrated, and composition-aware. It should read like a quiet sign-off, not a pasted sticker or separate footer card.
- Never place the logo on a hard white or solid rectangular backing, chip, pill, badge, banner, or floating tile. If contrast is needed, prefer a subtle tonal footer band or local contrast already belonging to the poster.
- Distinguish between style preferences and mandatory compliance.
- When brand guidance conflicts with the brief, selected playbook, or factual truth, keep the higher-precedence rule.
